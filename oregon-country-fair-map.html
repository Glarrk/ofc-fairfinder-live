About this map

A searchable, filterable guide layered on the official 2026 Oregon Country Fair grounds map (cartography by McKenzie Maps & PittsWebb / OCF Cartography Crew), stitched from the 2026 Peach Pit. Tap a marker — or search and filter at right — and the map flies to it.

Every stage, neighborhood, service and food booth is placed at its real cell on the map's printed grid (rows A–N, columns 1–9) exactly as published in the 2026 Peach Pit. The craft & artisan directory (500+ makers) is searchable by name, category and booth number; because the Peach Pit lists crafts by booth number rather than grid cell, those are a directory only — find the booth number on the base map. A few markers are tagged “approx.” where a cell was ambiguous.

🌻 Welcome to the Fair

Your offline pocket guide to the Oregon Country Fair, July 10–12. Everything here works with no signal.

  • Tap the map — pins open details; hit Navigate here for walking directions with voice.
  • Bottom menu — Explore places, open Saved for favorites & your pins, and browse Events with reminders.
  • Locate shows where you are. Tap the ☆ on any place to save it, or drop pins for your car, camp or a meet-up.
  • More — set your fair name & spirit animal, switch theme, go full screen, calibrate GPS, or change the narration voice.

New pin

Name it and pick an icon. It saves on this phone.

Fair name & spirit

Pick your fair name and a spirit animal — it becomes your marker on the map.

GPS calibration

The map is hand-drawn, so your dot needs anchoring to reality. Anchors are numbered green dots on the map.

Narration voice

Pick the voice for spoken directions. It defaults to a male voice; ♂ male and ♀ female are tagged where the device reports it. The available voices come from your phone or browser.
